Course Overview

This blended learning module introduces GS1 standards and explores the critical aspects of data interoperability in modern business systems. Designed for business professionals and students, the course combines lectures, hands‑on labs, and case studies. A basic understanding of business processes and information systems is recommended.

Learning Modules

Module 1 Introduction to GS1 Standards

GTIN, GLN, SSCC, GIAI • Why universal identification matters

GS1 maintains the global identification standards that uniquely identify products, locations, and assets; capture them in barcodes/RFID; and share them across trading partners. Adopting GS1 reduces duplication, supports automation, and improves traceability.

Module 2 EPCIS 2.0 — Visibility Events

What • Where • When • Why • AssociationEvent

  • Consistent data model + capture/query APIs across enterprises.
  • Event types: Object, Aggregation, Transaction, Transformation, Association.
  • Reduces integration costs and enables end‑to‑end traceability.
Module 3 GS1 Digital Link

From 1D to 2D barcodes • Resolvers • Omnichannel UX

Embed product identifiers in web URIs to bridge physical and digital worlds. Power recalls, authenticity, regulatory info, and consumer experiences from a single 2D symbol.

Module 4 Foundations of Interoperability

Legal • Organisational • Semantic • Technical

Interoperability spans laws and contracts, governance, shared meaning, and protocols. Learn how to assess maturity and design for cross‑border data flows under GDPR and similar regulations.

Module 5 Non‑Technical Aspects

Change • Governance • Economics

Culture, process alignment, skills, and incentives matter. Build stakeholder consensus and a governance model that survives real‑world constraints.

Module 6 Semantics & Interoperability

Vocabularies • Ontologies • Knowledge Graphs

Go beyond syntax. Use shared vocabularies, taxonomies, and ontologies to ensure meaning is preserved across systems. Learn practical techniques for schema mapping and ontology alignment.

Module 7 Standards & Protocols

REST vs SOAP • OAuth 2.1 • FRAND

Choose the right transport and security patterns for your context, and understand the IP/licensing guardrails that shape adoption.

Module 8 Reference Model Agro (rmAgro)

Agrifood data models • Traceability • Sustainability

Use rmAgro to standardise flows, test against use‑cases, and support AI‑ready data in crop, livestock, safety, and sustainability domains.

Module 9 Semantic Modelling in Composer

RDF/OWL tooling • Governance • APIs

Model, manage, and publish controlled vocabularies and ontologies with enterprise‑grade governance and API distribution.

Module 10 Semantic Modelling in Protégé

WebProtégé • OWL 2 • Collaborative editing

Build and reason over ontologies in open‑source tools; enable distributed collaboration with versioned change history.

Module 11 Linked Data & Ontologies

URIs • RDF/SPARQL • Knowledge graphs

Apply web principles to connect data across domains and make implicit assumptions explicit for AI/ML applications.

Final Capstone Project

Choose one pathway

  • Supply‑Chain Visibility System: Prototype tracking with EPCIS + Digital Link.
  • Semantic Integration Platform: Build an ontology‑driven interop layer.
  • Industry‑Specific Strategy: A complete interoperability roadmap.

Deliverables: 15–20 page design, working PoC, presentation, and peer review.
